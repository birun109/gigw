Failure vs Corrected Example: Portrait Mode Restriction

This demonstration shows why forcing a single orientation on mobile forms is a failure and how to correct it.

Failure: Portrait Locked

Scenario: Form locked to portrait using meta tag:

<meta name="screen-orientation" content="portrait">
Compliance Issue: Users cannot rotate to landscape or zoom easily. This prevents accessibility for users with fixed mounts or low vision. Orientation lock is unnecessary.

Corrected: Responsive Form

Scenario: Same form, fully responsive and accessible in any orientation. No orientation meta tag used.

Compliance: Users can rotate device freely, zoom, or use screen magnifiers. Form fields are programmatically labeled and fully accessible.
