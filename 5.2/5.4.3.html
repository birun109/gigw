GIGW 3.0 Statement 5.4.3: Website Governance Policies

Analysis and Requirements

**Requirement Focus**Mandatory definition and approval of ten key policies/plans.
**Approval Authority**Web Information Manager (WIM).
**Content Lifecycle Policies**
  • CMAP (Contribution, Moderation, Approval) Policy
  • CAP (Content Archival) Policy
  • CRP (Content Review) Policy
**Legal/Usage Policies**
  • Copyright Policy
  • Privacy Policy
  • Terms & Conditions
  • Hyperlinking Policy
**Infrastructure/Continuity Plans**
  • Security Policy
  • Contingency Management Plan
  • Website Monitoring Plan

Example for Success Criteria ✅

Success Scenario: Complete Policy Framework

Action: The WIM confirms that all ten required policies/plans are documented, officially approved, and their links are available:

  • **CMAP Policy:** Defines a workflow where content is drafted, reviewed by the Section Officer, approved by the Joint Secretary (WIM), and published by the Web Master.
  • **CAP Policy:** States that all content older than 3 years will be automatically moved to a designated archive server.
  • **Contingency Management Plan:** Details step-by-step procedures for handling server defacement and natural calamities, including contact lists and data restoration procedures.

The citizen-facing policies (Copyright, Privacy, T&C) are linked in the website footer.

**Compliance Justification:** All ten specific documents mandated by GIGW 5.4.3 are in place, approved by the WIM, and documented for lifecycle implementation. This is fully compliant.

Example for Failure Criteria ❌

Failure Scenario: Missing Key Plans

Action: The website governance documents are reviewed:

  • **Present:** Privacy Policy, Copyright Policy, and Security Policy.
  • **Missing:** The **Content Review (CRP) Policy**, **Content Archival (CAP) Policy**, and the **Website Monitoring Plan** do not exist as standalone, approved documents.

**Compliance Justification:** The statement requires **all** ten specific policies/plans to be clearly defined and approved. The absence of just one, such as the **CAP policy** (which defines how old content is handled), results in a failure to maintain the content lifecycle management structure. This fails GIGW 5.4.3.

Summary of Conformity

GIGW Requirement Key Mandate
5.4.3 Website Governance Policies Mandate that the WIM defines and approves the **full suite of ten policies/plans** (covering Content Lifecycle, Legal Usage, and Infrastructure Continuity). Absence of any one of these specified documents constitutes a failure.
