GIGW 3.0 Statement 5.4.4: Hyperlinking Accuracy and Indication

Analysis and Requirements

**Requirement Focus**Accuracy check and clear indication for external links.
**Government Action**Regularly check accuracy of linked content, and define the Hyperlinking Policy.
**Developer Action**Implement an alert mechanism when a link leads to a non-government website.
**Key Benefit**Mitigate visitor ambiguity about content ownership and ensure linked information is reliable.

Example for Success Criteria ✅

Success Scenario 1: External Link Indication

Action: The developer implements an icon and a screen reader-friendly attribute for all external links.

Visual and Programmatic Indication:

<a href="http://external-partner.com" target="_blank" title="Opens external website in new window"> Partner Portal <img src="external-icon.png" alt="(External link)"> </a>

Upon clicking, a small pop-up message appears: "You are about to leave the government domain and will be directed to an external website. Do you wish to proceed?"

**Compliance Justification:** The presence of the icon, the title attribute, the screen reader text (`alt="(External link)"`), and the confirmation pop-up all fulfill the requirement to provide a **clear indication** that the link leads out of the government website.

Success Scenario 2: Regular Accuracy Check (Govt. Action)

Action: The Hyperlinking Policy (as per 5.4.3) states that all external links must be verified every **90 days** by a Content Contributor to ensure the linked page is still relevant and accurate. The WIM monitors the execution of this schedule.

**Compliance Justification:** A mechanism (scheduled review) is actively defined and implemented to **check the accuracy of hyperlinked content regularly**, meeting the government organization's responsibility.

Example for Failure Criteria ❌

Failure Scenario 1: Missing External Link Alert

Action: The website contains links to three different state government schemes and one university website. All links are standard blue text, opening in the same window, with no visual icons, pop-ups, or text to indicate they are leaving the current domain.

<a href="http://university-research.edu">Research Findings</a>

**Compliance Justification:** Failing to provide a clear indication (visual, auditory, or interactive) when a link leads to a non-government website creates ambiguity and violates the developer action. This fails GIGW 5.4.4.

Failure Scenario 2: Broken or Inaccurate Links

Action: An external link to an older policy document on a partner website leads to a "404 Page Not Found" error. The link has been broken for six months, as the WIM has no mechanism or schedule in place for link auditing.

**Compliance Justification:** The government organization is mandated to have a mechanism in place to **check the accuracy of hyperlinked content regularly**. A long-standing broken link demonstrates the absence or failure of this mechanism, failing GIGW 5.4.4.

Summary of Conformity

GIGW Requirement Key Mandate
5.4.4 Hyperlinking Accuracy and Indication **Dual Requirement:** 1) The organization must **regularly verify the accuracy** of all linked content. 2) The developer must provide a **clear indication/alert** (e.g., icon, text, pop-up) to the user whenever a hyperlink leads away from the government domain to an external website.
