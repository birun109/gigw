GIGW 3.0 Statement 5.4.6: Simultaneous Multilingual Updates

Analysis and Requirements

**Requirement Focus**Content consistency across multiple language versions.
**Mandate**Documents/pages must be updated simultaneously.
**Government Action**Establish process to translate all content simultaneously with the original content creation.
**Developer Action**
  • CMS supports **multiple language publishing**.
  • CMS displays **status of non-translated content**.
**Key Benefit**Avoids inconsistencies and ensures all visitors receive the same, accurate information.

Example for Success Criteria ✅

Success Scenario 1: Enforced Simultaneous Workflow

Action: The CMS implements a "Translation Queue" and "Publish Lock" feature.

  • When the **English** version of a new circular is approved by the WIM, the content is automatically flagged as **Pending Translation** for Hindi and Telugu.
  • The CMS's master publishing button remains **disabled** until the Hindi and Telugu fields are marked as **Translated** and approved by the respective language editors.
  • Once all versions are approved, the content is published to all language sites *at the exact same time*.

**Compliance Justification:** The technical mechanism (Publish Lock) actively prevents non-simultaneous publishing, and the content status (Translated/Pending) is clearly tracked, meeting both the government and developer actions. This is fully compliant with GIGW 5.4.6.

Example for Failure Criteria ❌

Failure Scenario 1: Non-Simultaneous Update

Action: A major policy update is published on the **English** website at 10:00 AM. Due to reliance on manual translation and upload, the **Hindi** version is published four hours later at 2:00 PM.

**Compliance Justification:** The content versions were **not updated simultaneously**. For four hours, citizens accessing the Hindi version received outdated policy information, creating a clear inconsistency. This time gap, even if small, constitutes a failure of the mandatory simultaneous update requirement of GIGW 5.4.6.

Failure Scenario 2: Missing Status Display

Action: The CMS allows users to save content in different language fields, but there is no mechanism to visually display or track which languages are complete and which are pending translation. A moderator accidentally publishes the English version, unaware that the Hindi and Tamil fields are empty or contain only draft text.

**Compliance Justification:** The developer failed to provide a mechanism to **display the status of non-translated content**. This lack of status tracking directly leads to the failure of the simultaneous publishing mandate, failing GIGW 5.4.6.

Summary of Conformity

GIGW Requirement Key Mandate
5.4.6 Simultaneous Multilingual Updates The organization must ensure that **all language versions of a document/page are updated and published at the same time**. The CMS must support multilingual content and provide a mechanism to track and display the **status of translation** to prevent publication of incomplete or inconsistent content.
