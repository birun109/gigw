GIGW 3.0 Statement 5.4.8: No 'Under Construction' Pages

Analysis and Requirements

**Requirement Focus**Prohibition of incomplete pages or placeholder links.
**Mandate**There are **no links** to pages marked ‘under construction’.
**Developer Action**Avoid publishing pages with **"under construction" / "work in progress"** text.
**Event-based Content**If content is event-based and not yet ready, provide an **expected date** of availability.
**Key Benefit**Ensures a better user experience and avoids conveying a negative impression of the website's status.

Example for Success Criteria ✅

Success Scenario 1: Pre-launch Content Management

Action: The web team is preparing a new section on "Digital Initiatives" but the content is not yet complete.

  • **Correct Approach:** The link to the "Digital Initiatives" page is **temporarily removed** from the main navigation and sitemap until the page is 100% complete and approved.

Success Scenario 2: Event-Based Content

The page for the upcoming "Annual Budget Presentation Documents" is not yet available.

  • **Correct Approach:** The link is live, but the page content states: "Documents related to the Annual Budget Presentation will be made available here immediately following the presentation on **February 1, 20XX**."

**Compliance Justification:** By removing incomplete links or, for temporary/event content, providing a **definite date of availability**, the website avoids forcing the visitor to view a frustrating "under construction" notice. This is fully compliant with GIGW 5.4.8.

Example for Failure Criteria ❌

Failure Scenario 1: Placeholder Page

Action: A user clicks a menu item labeled "Our History" and is directed to a page displaying only the large, centered text: **"This Page is Under Construction. Please Check Back Soon."**

**Compliance Justification:** The explicit presence of the phrase "Under Construction" or "Work in Progress" violates the mandatory developer action and fails the statement's primary objective. This is a clear failure of GIGW 5.4.8.

Failure Scenario 2: Indefinite Availability

Action: A link to "Future Schemes" directs the user to a page that states: "Details of future schemes will be posted here when they are finalized."

**Compliance Justification:** While the message avoids the exact phrase "under construction," it provides an **indefinite** commitment (no date or timeline). For content that is not yet ready, the government organization must provide a date. This indefinite placeholder violates the spirit and action required by GIGW 5.4.8.

Summary of Conformity

GIGW Requirement Key Mandate
5.4.8 No 'Under Construction' Pages **Never link to or publish pages containing "under construction" or "work in progress" messages.** If content is not ready, either remove the link entirely until the content is approved, or provide a **clear, expected date of publication** for event-based or time-sensitive content.
