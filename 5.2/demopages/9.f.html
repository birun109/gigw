GIGW 3.0 – Clause 5.2.9

Failure Examples – Reliance on Sensory Characteristics ❌

Failure Example 1: Relying Only on Shape

Scenario: Instruction tells the user to click a button using only its shape.

To proceed to the next step, click the round button.

You clicked the button.

Why This Fails:
The instruction relies solely on the sensory characteristic (shape – "round") and does not provide a textual identifier. A screen reader user cannot perceive shape, making the instruction inaccessible.

Failure Example 2: Relying Only on Location

Scenario: Instruction guides the user based only on position.

Application Review Section

To finalize your application, click the button in the lower right corner.

Application submitted successfully.

Why This Fails:
The instruction depends only on the sensory characteristic (location – "lower right corner") and does not mention the button's label. Screen reader users or users with visual impairments cannot determine which button to activate.
