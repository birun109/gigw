GIGW 3.0 – Clause 5.2.9

Success Examples – Not Relying Solely on Sensory Characteristics ✅

Success Example 1: Shape + Textual Label

Scenario: Instructions tell the user to click a specific button based on its shape and text.

To proceed to the next step, click the round button labelled "GO".

You clicked the GO button.

Compliance Justification:
The instruction does not rely solely on the sensory characteristic (shape, "round"). It provides the necessary textual identifier ("GO"), making the instruction accessible to screen reader users who cannot see the shape.

Success Example 2: Location + Textual Identification

Scenario: Instructions guide the user to a button based on its general position and a clear label.

Application Review Section

To finalize your application, click the button labelled "SUBMIT FINAL" in the lower right corner.

Application submitted successfully.

Compliance Justification:
Although it mentions location ("lower right corner"), the primary means of identifying the target is the clear and unique textual label ("SUBMIT FINAL"). The location information is supplementary.
