GIGW 3.0 Overview

⚠ This webpage is created purely for training and educational demonstration purposes to understand the structure, implementation, and compliance requirements of GIGW 3.0. It is not an official Government of India website.

1. What is GIGW 3.0?

Guidelines for Indian Government Websites (GIGW) 3.0 is a framework issued by the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), Government of India. It standardizes government websites to ensure accessibility, usability, security, interoperability, and citizen-centric digital service delivery.

2. Reference Standards, Laws & Guidelines

  • GIGW 3.0 Guidelines – Source: MeitY, Government of India
  • WCAG 2.1 – Web Content Accessibility Guidelines – Source: W3C
  • Information Technology Act, 2000 – Source: MeitY, Government of India
  • Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 – Source: Department of Empowerment of Persons with Disabilities
  • CERT-In Cyber Security Guidelines – Source: Indian Computer Emergency Response Team
  • ISO/IEC Web Standards – Source: International Organization for Standardization

4. Why GIGW 3.0 is Required in Government Sector

  • Ensures uniformity across government websites
  • Provides inclusive digital access to all citizens
  • Enhances transparency and accountability
  • Improves cybersecurity posture
  • Supports Digital India vision

5. Risks if Not Followed

  • Legal non-compliance under IT Act and RPwD Act
  • Accessibility complaints and litigation
  • Cybersecurity breaches and data compromise
  • Loss of citizen trust
  • Audit failure during compliance review

6. How to Implement GIGW 3.0

  1. Understand GIGW clauses and compliance requirements
  2. Conduct gap analysis and accessibility audit
  3. Implement WCAG 2.1 accessibility features
  4. Ensure adherence to open standards and security policies
  5. Publish compliance statement
  6. Perform periodic audits and monitoring

7. Key Terms Used in GIGW

  • Accessibility: Designing digital content usable by persons with disabilities
  • Compliance: Meeting mandatory guidelines
  • Audit: Systematic evaluation of conformity
  • Responsive Design: Adapting website layout across devices
  • Interoperability: Systems working seamlessly together
  • Metadata: Structured information describing content
